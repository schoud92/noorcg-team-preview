Accessibility Statement

We build software for a living, and we hold our own site to the same standard we set for our clients. This is our commitment to making this website usable by everyone, what we have done to get there, and how to reach us if something falls short.

LEGAL · LAST UPDATED JUNE 2026

Noor Consulting Group ("Noor," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to making this website accessible to as many people as possible, regardless of ability, device, or assistive technology. Accessibility is not a box we tick at the end of a project — it is part of how we design and build, both for our clients and for ourselves.

1. The Standard We Target

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA , published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines are the widely accepted benchmark for accessible web content, and they are the same standard we recommend to the organizations we work with. Conformance is an ongoing effort rather than a one-time certification: as we update the site, we measure new and changed content against these criteria.

2. Measures We Take

We have built this site with accessibility in mind, and we maintain it the same way. Specific measures include:

Semantic HTML — pages are structured with meaningful landmarks, headings in a logical order, and native elements (buttons, links, lists) so that screen readers and other assistive technologies can interpret the content correctly.

  • Keyboard navigation — interactive elements are reachable and operable with a keyboard alone, with a visible focus indicator so you always know where you are on the page.
  • Reduced motion — we honor the prefers-reduced-motion system setting, so animations and transitions are minimized for visitors who have asked their device to limit motion.
  • ARIA labeling — where native semantics are not sufficient, we add ARIA roles and labels to give controls and icons the right meaning, and we hide purely decorative elements from assistive technology.
  • Color and contrast — we pay deliberate attention to the contrast between text and its background, and we avoid relying on color alone to convey meaning.
  • Responsive, resilient layout — content reflows across screen sizes and remains usable when text is enlarged or the page is zoomed.

3. Known Limitations

We would rather be honest about where gaps may remain than claim a perfect score. Despite our efforts, some parts of this site may not yet fully meet our target standard:

  • Interactive demos — the live product demonstrations embedded on this site are simplified illustrations of client work, and some interactive states within them may not be fully optimized for assistive technology.
  • Third-party embeds — content served by external providers (such as embedded media, maps, or analytics widgets) is not always within our direct control, and its accessibility may vary.

Where we identify a barrier, we prioritize fixing it. If one of these limitations prevents you from using the site, please tell us — we will work with you directly to provide the information or function you need in an alternative form.

4. Requesting Accommodations or Reporting an Issue

If you encounter an accessibility barrier on this site, need information in an alternative format, or would like to request an accommodation, contact us at info@noorcg.com . Where possible, include the page address, a short description of the problem, and the browser and assistive technology you are using — that detail helps us reproduce and resolve the issue quickly.

We aim to acknowledge accessibility requests within two business days and to provide a meaningful response or a workaround as quickly as we reasonably can. We treat this feedback as a priority, not a formality.

5. Ongoing Commitment

Accessibility is a moving target, and we treat it as continuous work. We review the site as it evolves, test against assistive technologies, and update this statement when our practices or the underlying standards change. If you have suggestions for how we can do better, we genuinely want to hear them.
